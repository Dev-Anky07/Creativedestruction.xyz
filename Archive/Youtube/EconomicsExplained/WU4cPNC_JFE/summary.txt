- The health of the economy also surely impacts the companies that are listed in all of these exchanges around the world. Can the economy teach us what the stock market is going to do, or vice versa? If it can, what are the indicators saying right now?
- Finding a correlation between two sets of economic data is something that consumes the lives of people in the field of econometrics. The performance of the economy accounts for around 7% to 11% of the growth or contraction of the stock market. Other variables will stand between the desired outcome and the mechanism by which that outcome is achieved.
- If an investor thinks the economy is going to do poorly, they might sell short term bonds and buy long term bonds. People betting against the economy can profit off this by buying bonds at a higher interest rate before they think rates will drop and then sell them at a profit if rates do actually drop.
