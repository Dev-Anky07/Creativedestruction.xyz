- By 2050, most economic projections have the US. as the third or fourth most productive country in the world, behind China, India and potentially Indonesia. Some economists argue that the USA should take drastic actions, like closing itself off to outside trade and defaulting on its debt, to maintain its economic dominance.
- Global economic output is growing at the fastest rate in history. But the benefits have been heavily concentrated into just a few groups. The largest group that has lost out to globalization has been nontechnical manufacturing workers in advanced economies. Another loser has been the US. Federal government itself.
- The USA and other advanced Western economies owe a lot of money to developing economies. Household debt has also become an issue accelerated by income disparities. The most sensible solution would be to encourage investment over consumption. In addition, the US. should enact policies that protect local industries.
