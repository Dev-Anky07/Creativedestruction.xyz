- Japan was the first of the modern Asian countries to experience sustained economic growth. After the end of World War II, Japan was able to rebuild with the help of the Allies. But Japan has seen no real growth in the past 30 years. There are a few big causes to this slowdown.
- Japan is experiencing deflation, meaning that things are costing less and less every single year. To control any economy, the government has two main control mechanisms. These are monetary policy and fiscal policy. While central banks can help stimulate an economy, this is not their primary function.
